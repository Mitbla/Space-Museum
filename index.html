SUN

The star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core. The Sun radiates energy mainly as visible light, ultraviolet light, and infrared radiation, and is the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

OSIRIS-REX

NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission successfully returned a capsule to Earth containing pristine material gathered from the near-Earth asteroid Bennu. Initial analysis of the 4.5-billion-year-old sample reveals high carbon content and water, shedding light on the origins of life and our solar system.

One Museum, Infinite Wonders

The Online Space Museum, located in the digital ether, is a curated collection of planetary bodies and celestial phenomena. It houses detailed records and high-resolution captures of the most awe-inspiring artifacts in our solar system and beyond.

Dr. M. Explorer

Dr. Explorer is the Chief Curator of Planetary Artifacts and a supervisory astronomer in the Astrophysics Department.

They are responsible for the curation of the "Milestones of the Solar System" hall and served as the co-lead curator for that exhibit's latest digital iteration.
